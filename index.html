SBA 0001 (Traumatic brain injury)
An 42 year old man presents to a DGH following a traumatic head injury. His CT head scan is normal, His GCS is 7 and he had abnormal motor posturing. He is not currently sedated. His unsupported systolic blood pressure in 110/60 (MAP 77). He had one short self resolving seizure post injury 2 hours ago
According to British trauma foundation which criteria does he meet for ICP monitoring?

A Age over 25 with GCS less than 8
B GCS <8 with abnormal motor posturing and age over 40
C GCS <8 with a normal CT head scan
D Seizure activity post injury
E TBI with Mean arterial pressure below 80

Click to reveal answer + Explanation B GCS <8 with abnormal motor posturing and age over 40

BTF 2016 TBI guidlines have the following criteria for ICP monitoring
- GCS <12 who cannot not be serially assess due to sedation
- GCS <8 with and abnormal CT head
- GCS <8 with with an normal scan if 2 of the following present: Age>40, SBP <90, Abdnormal motor posturing

There is no accounting for seizure activity.

SBA 0012 Thrombocytopenia
An 45 year old women is on ICU following a complicated orthopaedic procedure. She has been on a Unfractionated heparin infusion for 7 days but despite this develops a DVT. She does not have signs of sepsis and her fluid balance in neutral since admission to intensive care.
Blood results show
Hb 95
WCC 12
Plt 35 (Baseline 90)

What would be her 4 T score and what would be the coorect treatment

A 8, Stop heparin and anticoagulate with non heparin alternative
B 8. Stop Heparin and no futher anticoagulation due to low platelets
C 4, Stop heparin and start Warfarin for the DVT
D 4, Stop heparin and anticoagulate with non heparin alternative
E 8, Stop heparin and start prophylactic dose fondaparinux

Click to reveal answer + Explanation
A 8, Stop heparin and anticoagulate with non heparin alternative

A similar question has been in the MCQ in 2024. This case is supposed to describe HIT. They score maximum points on the 4T score with 2 points for the extent of the thrombocytopenia, 2 for the timing, 2 for having throbosis and 2 for there not being a possible alternative. In this case treatment needs to be started by stoping heparin and anticoagulating with an alternative depending on patient factors. Warfarin is contraindicated until platelet count has normalised.
Type II HIT (as in this case) is casued by immune response to PF4/Heparin complexes. The antibodies can be detected on bloods but can take several days to come back and still need to be interpreted with the clinical picture.

SBA 0025 Parkland Formula for Burns
A rugby player was attending his clubs summer barbeque when he was badly burnt when trying to light a bonfire. He is 100kg with 50% total body surface area full thickness burns. The Paramedics gave him 1000mL NaCl 0.9% and ED have also given 1000mL of Plasmalyte.

According to the Parkland Formula, How much further fluid should he receive in the 8 hrs following the burn
A 4,000 mL
B 6,000 mL
C 8,000 mL
D 10,000 mL
E 20,000 mL

Click to reveal answer + Explanation
C 8,000 mL

It is not clear whether following parkland formular is the correct method to follow with burns resucitation, regardless this is an easy question and has come up previously.
The Modified Parkland Formula recommends administering 4 mL/kg/% burn for the first 24 hours, with half of the total calculated volume given in the first 8 hours. For this patient (100kg with 50% burns), the calculation would be: 4*100*50 = 20,000ml. Half of this (10,000 mL) should be administered in the first 8 hours for resuscitation. The patient received 2 litres prior to your review leaving 8 litres to be given in the first 8 hours.




SBA 0026 Troubleshooting the dialysis circuit
A 45 year old with vasopressor resistant septic shock and renal failure secondary to Group A streptococcus infection is on your unit. He was previously well but is reaching the limits of ICU care. There is no availability of VA ECMO in your region. You are asked to review him on your night shift because the renal replacement machine is constantly alarming.

C Misplaced Vascath
D Hydroxycobalamin

Click to reveal answer + Explanation
D Hydroxycobalamin

Hydroxycobalamin can be used is septic shock as a nitric oxide scavenger when other methods have not worked. It leads to a discolouration of all body fluids and can cause the blood leak alarm. A Metabolic Alkalosis is incorrect as it indicates citrate overload rather than toxicity, the alkalosis indicates the citrate is being metabolised by the liver leading to alkalosis from the remaining Na+. B is incorrect as citrate toxicity gives a High anion gap metabolic acidosis. C is incorrect and the total calcium to ionised calcium ratio would be high in citrate toxicity.
